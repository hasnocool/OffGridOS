# 12. Governance and Contribution Standards

## Decision principles

Prioritize safety, user ownership, offline utility, interoperability, repairability, accessibility, and transparent economics. A feature that increases dependence or physical risk needs stronger evidence and review.

## Contribution flow

Use issues for proposals and bugs, small focused changes, clear commit messages, reproducible tests, and documentation updates alongside behavior changes. Security-sensitive reports should use a private disclosure path when available.

## Review expectations

Reviews should check correctness, failure behavior, privacy, resource usage, accessibility, migration impact, and whether the change works without internet. Safety-critical or financial features require domain review before release.

## Versioning and deprecation

Use semantic versioning for public APIs and documented migration notes for data changes. Deprecations require a notice period, an export path, and a supported alternative where practical.

## Community conduct

Participation must be respectful and inclusive. Harassment, fraud, doxxing, unsafe instructions, and manipulation of reputation or credit systems are not acceptable. Governance rules may evolve through documented proposals and community feedback.
