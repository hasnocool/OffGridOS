# 10. Revenue and Go-to-Market

## Entry market

Start with technically motivated off-grid households, RV users, homesteaders, remote workers, and preparedness communities. They have clear needs, can provide direct feedback, and value local reliability.

## Offers

- OMS software and supported images
- Tested hardware bundles
- Installation and system design
- Backup, migration, and maintenance services
- Training and documentation packages
- Community network deployments
- Optional support and hosted synchronization

## Distribution

Use direct online distribution, installers and repair shops, community demonstrations, maker and homesteading groups, and partnerships with renewable-energy and rural-service providers.

## Trust strategy

Publish power measurements, supported hardware, limitations, security practices, repair instructions, pricing, and data policies. Avoid locking core functionality behind subscriptions.

## Metrics

Track successful installations, offline uptime, restore success, support burden, energy use, retention, paid conversion, customer referrals, and community service utilization. Revenue growth must not come at the expense of reliability or user ownership.
