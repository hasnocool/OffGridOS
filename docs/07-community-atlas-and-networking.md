# 7. Community Atlas and Networking

## Purpose

Atlas helps people discover trusted locations, services, resources, and safe access arrangements while allowing landowners and businesses to define boundaries and participation terms.

## Location record

Each listing can include approximate or exact location, access conditions, operating hours, offered services, capacity, accessibility, contact method, fee or exchange terms, safety notes, reputation history, verification status, and last-updated date.

## Privacy and safety

Sensitive locations should support coarse coordinates, invite-only visibility, delayed publication, and operator-controlled fields. Listings must not expose private details without consent. Safety ratings are signals, not guarantees; users need clear disclaimers and emergency guidance.

## Network modes

Atlas data can synchronize over local Wi-Fi, community mesh, intermittent internet, removable media, or approved relay nodes. A node should be able to publish only selected records and revoke access.

## Moderation

Use evidence-based reports, transparent status changes, appeals, anti-spam controls, and separation between factual verification and subjective reputation. No single rating should determine access to essential services.
