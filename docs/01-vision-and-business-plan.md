# 1. Vision and Business Plan

## Executive summary

OffGridOS is an offline-first technology platform for people and communities that need dependable access to information, media, computing, communication, and local services despite unreliable connectivity, high infrastructure costs, or remote locations.

The initial product is the OffGridOS Media Server (OMS), a small, efficient local server that provides useful services over a local network without requiring a cloud account. Later products extend the same foundation into energy systems, community mapping, local AI, resilient communications, and a trusted service network.

## Problem

Remote households, vehicle dwellers, homesteaders, emergency groups, and small communities often depend on fragile internet services, expensive subscriptions, incompatible equipment, and inaccessible technical knowledge. Power and bandwidth constraints make conventional cloud-first software a poor fit.

## Customers

Primary customers are off-grid households, RV and tent-trailer users, remote workers, homesteaders, preparedness groups, small farms, rural businesses, community networks, and organizations supporting disaster resilience.

## Business model

Revenue can come from hardware bundles, installation, support subscriptions, managed updates, optional hosted synchronization, training, community deployments, marketplace transactions, and partnerships. Core local functionality remains useful without a subscription.

## Competitive advantage

The platform is purpose-built around low power, local ownership, graceful degradation, repairability, open interfaces, and a coherent product ecosystem. A common software base reduces deployment complexity while allowing different hardware and Linux editions.

## Success measures

Phase 1 success means a reliable install, documented hardware targets, useful offline services, safe update and backup workflows, and a small group of real users who can operate the system without specialist assistance.
